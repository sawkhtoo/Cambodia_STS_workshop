Background

Cambodia has been recommended for graduation from the Least Developed Country (LDC) category by the UN Committee for Development Policy (CDP) in 2024. This recommendation was endorsed by ECOSOC, and the General Assembly took note of it, granting an exceptional five-year preparatory period. Cambodia is scheduled to graduate in December 2029.

To facilitate a smooth transition, Cambodia has initiated several measures, including preparation of a Smooth Transition Strategy (STS) and steps to mitigate potential impacts of graduation. The government has begun institutional arrangements and development of a transition roadmap, incorporating input from potentially affected stakeholders.

In this context, a regional workshop is organized to learn from the experiences of other countries that have recently graduated or are in the process of graduating, and to support preparation and implementation of the STS.

Key details

Dates: 30–31 October 2025

Location: Phnom Penh, Cambodia

Format: Onsite workshop with presentations, discussions, and knowledge exchange

Note: Invited speakers are to be confirmed.

Objectives

  • Provide Cambodia exposure to experiences of graduated and graduating countries (e.g., Bangladesh, Bhutan, Lao PDR, Nepal, Senegal) and UN entities supporting graduating countries.
  • Deliver key information on LDC graduation and smooth transition strategies.
  • Facilitate connections with relevant institutions to ensure an effective and timely preparation process.

Target audience

The target group are government officials at line ministries and other institutions expected to lead the STS and/or working in areas likely to be affected by LDC graduation. The event will host ~100 participants, including ~10 international attendees and ~10 representatives from UN entities.
