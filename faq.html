Workshop FAQs – Phnom Penh, Cambodia

General Information

Q: When and where will the workshop take place?

A: The workshop will be held on 30–31 October 2025 in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Participants are expected to arrive on 29 October and depart on 1 November.

Q: What is the format of the workshop?

A: The event will be conducted onsite and will include technical presentations and working sessions.

Registration

Q: Do I need to register?

A: Yes. All participants must register in advance. A registration link will be shared with confirmed participants.

Travel & Sponsorship

Q: What are my travel entitlements?

A: Sponsored participants are entitled to:

  • Daily Subsistence Allowance (DSA): USD 157 per night, payable for 3 nights (29–31 October). DSA is not payable for the departure day (1 November).
  • Travel Advance: Approximately 75% of total DSA will be issued prior to departure.
  • Terminal Expenses (TE): USD 252, payable upon completion of the mission.
  • Remaining DSA Balance: Paid after submission of required travel documentation (e.g., boarding passes).

Q: In what currency will payments be made?

A: All UN payments are made in USD. The UN does not adjust for any loss or gain arising from local bank currency conversions.

Q: How will I receive my DSA and TE?

A: Participants must inform the UN in advance of their preferred payment method and submit the UN standard bank information form. Payments can be made via direct transfer to your bank account (if it can receive UN payments). If there are restrictions, you may request to collect funds in USD through the UNDP Office in Cambodia.

Accommodation

Q: Will the UN book my hotel?

A: No. Participants are responsible for booking their own accommodation. The DSA is provided to cover lodging, meals, and incidental expenses.

Transportation

Q: Will airport transfers be arranged?

A: No. Participants are responsible for arranging their own transport between the airport, hotel, and workshop venue. Terminal Expenses (TE) are provided to cover these costs.

Entry Visas

Q: Do I need a visa for Cambodia?

A: Participants are required to secure their own visas, if applicable. If you need a support letter, please contact the organizers in advance.

Workshop Practicalities

Q: What language will the workshop be conducted in?

A: The workshop will be held in English, with interpretation services provided.

Q: What is the dress code?

A: Business casual.

Q: Are meals included?

A: Coffee breaks and lunches will be provided at the venue. Other meals are at participants’ own expense (covered by DSA).

Q: What expenses are not covered?

A: The UN does not cover travel/health insurance, medical costs, personal expenses, or compensation for loss/damage to property.

Local Information

Q: What currency is used in Cambodia?

A: The Cambodian Riel (KHR) is the official currency, but US dollars are widely accepted.

Q: What is the local time zone?

A: Phnom Penh is in GMT+7.

Q: What type of electrical plugs are used?

A: Cambodia mainly uses types A, C, and G. Please bring an adapter if needed.

Q: What weather should I expect in late October?

A: Phnom Penh in late October is warm and humid, with temperatures around 25–30°C. Light clothing and an umbrella are recommended.
